Practical Information

Getting There

  • Parking on site will be available.
  • Shuttle service will not be provided. Everyone will be travelling from and staying in different directions, and unfortunately it is beyond our means to organise this logistically.
  • Uber / Bolt / taxi works well here. We'll be just outside Prague and ride-sharing works well in the area. For a reasonable price (low hundreds of CZK) you can get to the outskirts of Prague or nearby villages. If you have trouble calling a taxi, we're happy to help.

The Venue

The wedding will take place mostly outdoors. There is plenty of outdoor space. We have tents and a marquee ready in case of rain. We still recommend bringing spare, warmer shoes in case it rains.

Accommodation

Unfortunately we are unable to arrange accommodation for everyone — it will only be provided for immediate family. Others have the following options:

  • Large shared room A large shared room (in the attic) where you can lay out your sleeping mat and sleeping bag.
  • Camping in the garden There is truly plenty of space and enough facilities (WC, bathroom) for changing and freshening up. Tent space is plentiful. Tents may be set up any time after the toast on Saturday. If you plan to arrive on Friday, you can sleep in the shared room or the marquee.
  • Your own accommodation If you prefer more comfort, we recommend checking Booking.com or Airbnb.com for accommodation nearby. There are a few guesthouses in the surrounding villages.

Who to Bring

  • Children are welcome. We'll have two little ones with us ourselves. There will be plenty of space and activities for kids! Please note that parents are responsible for their children throughout the wedding. Childcare will not be provided.
  • Please leave four-legged friends at home. While we love dogs, there will be many children at the event and not all of them are comfortable around animals. We'd like to avoid any potential issues.

What to Bring

  • Good spirits!
  • An umbrella, wellies, a blanket, and warmer spare clothes and shoes. An umbrella is handy for rain or as a parasol. The ground is mostly grassy so wellies may come in useful. A blanket for warmth is also a good idea.
  • For children we recommend swimwear, floats, and outdoor toys. There is a fenced pool in the garden (extra vigilance for your children here!).
  • Sports equipment. Gear for outdoor sports like badminton, tried-and-tested outdoor games for children.
  • Anything fun — homemade spirits, musical instruments, homemade cakes, etc.
